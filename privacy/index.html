Privacy Policy

Last updated: 29 May 2026 · GDPR compliant

1. Data Controller

This is a personal blog operated as a private individual, not a commercial entity. There is no registered data protection officer (DPO) requirement under Art. 37 GDPR for personal processing at this scale.

2. What Data We Collect

2.1 Server Logs

Like any web server, Nginx logs every HTTP request. These logs contain your IP address, the requested URL, timestamp, browser user-agent string, and HTTP status code. Log files are retained for a maximum of 30 days and are not shared with third parties. They are used solely for security monitoring and debugging.

2.2 Session Cookies

This site uses a single technical session cookie for the admin panel. Public visitors receive no cookies from this site. There are no tracking cookies, no analytics cookies, and no third-party cookies on public pages.

The decryption key you enter to unlock encrypted content is stored in your browser's sessionStorage only — it is cleared when you close the tab and never sent to the server.

3. Legal Basis for Processing

Processing of server logs is based on Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR — the legitimate interest in maintaining the security and integrity of the service.

4. Data Sharing and Transfer

No personal data is sold, rented, or shared with third parties for marketing purposes. Server infrastructure is operated by the data controller directly.

5. Your Rights Under GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15) — you may request a copy of any personal data held about you.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16) — you may request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17) — you may request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten").
  • Right to restriction (Art. 18) — you may request that processing of your data be limited.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20) — you may request your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object (Art. 21) — you may object to processing based on legitimate interest.
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time.

6. Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe your data has been processed unlawfully, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In Germany, the competent authority is the Bundesbeauftragte für den Datenschutz und die Informationsfreiheit (BfDI) or the data protection authority of your federal state. In other EU member states, the relevant national DPA applies.

7. No Automated Decision-Making

This site does not use automated decision-making or profiling as defined in Art. 22 GDPR.

8. Links to Third-Party Sites

This blog may contain links to external websites. Once you leave this site, this privacy policy no longer applies. The operator has no control over and accepts no responsibility for the content or privacy practices of external sites.

9. Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. The date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. Continued use of the site after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the new terms.
